About

Background, education, and what I'm spending my spare cycles on.

I work as an infrastructure engineer supporting chemical and pharmaceutical manufacturing environments, where the network and the systems come in all flavors and the goal is have it behave the same way at 3am as they do at 3pm. That means a lot of time inside Windows and Linux Servers, Active Directory, Hyper-V, the switches and VLANs connecting it all together, plus the kind of troubleshooting where time can cost you a million dollars a day.

My background is a dual degree in Computer Science and Chemical Engineering from Rutgers, which turned out to be a strange but useful combination: process thinking from one side, systems thinking from the other.

Outside of work I'm running a home lab to push further into automation and orchestration tooling, training for the Niagara Falls Marathon, and slowly getting better at Magic: The Gathering than is probably healthy.
